Projects

Windchill Collaborative PLM - Pruning Shears Optimization

PLM · Windchill · CAD(Creo) · FEA/Ansys WB · Ashby material selection

Building on a pruning shears mechanism I designed, worked in a collaborative team using the Windchill PLM platform to optimize kinematic and fixed joints, refine part geometry via FEA (Ansys Workbench), select materials using the Ashby method and finalize part dimensioning and tolerances.

  • Managed the project collaboratively via Windchill (PLM) with a shared CAD tree/structure
  • Optimized kinematic joints: bearing lifetime calculations, bushing selection, eccentric/oblong slot connection
  • Sized fixed joints: pin shear calculations, keyway sizing, bolted gearbox-to-frame connection
  • Optimized part geometry (frame, oblong, eccentric) via Ansys stress analysis to reduce stress concentrations
  • Selected materials (aluminum, steel) using the Ashby method with Granta EduPack, balancing mass and cost

Vibration Measurement via Homodyne Interferometry

Optics · Michelson Interferometer · Resonance Frequencies

Designed and validated a proof-of-concept vibrometer based on a homodyne Michelson interferometer, using fringe displacement to detect the bending vibration modes of a cantilever beam, and compared measured resonance frequencies to theoretical predictions.

  • Conducted technology and literature review on laser vibrometry (homodyne, heterodyne, laser Doppler methods)
  • Designed and built a Michelson interferometer setup with polarization optics (waveplates, PBS cubes) and a piezo-driven mirror
  • Simulated fringe behavior in MATLAB for small vs. large vibration amplitudes relative to laser wavelength
  • Compared experimental resonance frequencies to theoretical beam-bending model, discussing sources of discrepancy

Ultrasound Simulation

MATLAB · Ultrasound Imaging · Signal Processing · Biomedical Simulation

Simulated the acoustic behavior of a breast ultrasound exam in MATLAB, modeling pulse propagation and echo reflection through layered tissue to detect a simulated lobular carcinoma in situ. Used time-of-flight estimation via cross-correlation and analyzed measurement robustness under varying noise conditions (SNR).

  • Modeled a 4-layer breast tissue model (skin, fat, gland, muscle) with acoustic impedance and propagation velocity
  • Simulated pulse emission and echo reflection with realistic time delays
  • Used cross-correlation to estimate time-of-flight between emitted and reflected signals
  • Analyzed measurement accuracy vs. SNR under added noise
  • Extended simulation to multi-pulse echo trains

Cleanroom Microfabrication

Microfabrication · Cleanroom · Photolithography

  • Practiced spin-coating and photoresist processing (S1813, AZ nLOF 2020, SU-8) with defined spin/bake parameters
